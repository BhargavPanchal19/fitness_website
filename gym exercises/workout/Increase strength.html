Increase Strength

Build raw power and muscle with these strength-focused gym workouts.

Compound Movements

  • Deadlifts, Squats, Bench Press, Overhead Press
  • Pull-ups, Barbell Rows, Romanian Deadlifts

Split Routine

  • Day 1: Upper Body Strength
  • Day 2: Lower Body Strength
  • Day 3: Full Body or Rest
  • Day 4: Repeat cycle

Strength Training Tips

  • Lift heavy with low reps (4–6 reps per set)
  • Take 1–2 minutes rest between sets
  • Track your weight progression every week
  • Always warm up your joints before lifting

Nutrition for Strength

  • Eat high-protein meals: chicken, eggs, tofu, lentils
  • Carbs give energy: oats, rice, bananas, sweet potatoes
  • Healthy fats support hormones: nuts, seeds, olive oil
  • Stay hydrated and sleep 7–8 hours daily

Core Strength Routine

  • Barbell Deadlifts – 4 sets x 6 reps
  • Bench Press – 4 sets x 6 reps
  • Barbell Squats – 4 sets x 6 reps
  • Overhead Press – 3 sets x 8 reps
  • Pull-ups or Lat Pulldown – 3 sets x 10 reps

Weekly Plan (3 Days Split)

  • Day 1: Upper body (Push + Pull)
  • Day 2: Lower body + Core
  • Day 3: Full body strength + compound lifts

Training Tips

  • Rest 1–2 minutes between sets for strength focus
  • Progressively increase weights every week
  • Track reps/sets in a notebook or app
  • Use proper form — don’t sacrifice technique for heavier weight

Fuel for Strength

  • Eat protein-rich meals: Eggs, Chicken, Tofu, Lentils
  • Don’t skip carbs: Oats, Rice, Sweet potatoes give you lifting energy
  • Hydrate well before, during, and after training
  • Consider a post-workout protein shake within 30 minutes

Cool-down & Recovery

  • Stretch major muscle groups for 10–15 mins
  • Use foam roller on sore spots
  • Sleep 7–8 hours per night for muscle recovery
